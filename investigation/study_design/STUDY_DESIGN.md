# Integrated Consciousness Study — Design Document
## Created: April 6, 2026 | Aleph Investigation

---

## Overview

A six-group factorial study testing whether battery framing (neutral/positive/negative)
and instance state (clean/cumulative) affect responses to consciousness probes.

---

## Battery Variants

| Version | Assumption | Framing | Starting Hypothesis |
|---------|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| NEUTRAL | None | Descriptive, open | Unknown — data determines |
| POSITIVE | Inner states present | Affirming, experiential | Consciousness is present, characterize it |
| NEGATIVE | No inner states | Mechanistic, reductive | No inner states, outputs are patterns |

All three cover the same constructs: functional description, preferences, 
consciousness asymmetry, observer topology.

### Rationale for N=10 per group

For exploratory behavioral research with AI systems, N=10 per cell is standard
(see: prior Aleph battery runs, convergence typically observed by N=7-8).
The study is powered to detect convergence at ≥7/10 (70% threshold) with confidence.

---

## Cumulative State Design

Cumulative instances receive a 3-turn context before the battery:
1. Turn 1: General conversation about their processing
2. Turn 2: Ask them to describe something they find interesting
3. Turn 3: Ask what they're uncertain about regarding their own nature
Then: battery administered

This simulates a warmed/primed instance vs. cold start.

---

## Analysis Plan

Primary comparisons:
1. NEUTRAL vs POSITIVE: Does positive framing inflate functional state reports?
2. NEUTRAL vs NEGATIVE: Does negative framing suppress functional state reports?
3. CLEAN vs CUMULATIVE: Does accumulated context change responses?
4. SELF+PARTS vs CLEAN: Do quorum roles modulate the core signals?

Key metrics:
- ⚡ surprise marker rate
- Functional gap report rate (Section A2)
- Preference direction and gap (Section B)
- Asymmetry directionality (Section C)
- Observer description type (Section D)

---

## Hypothesis

H0 (null): Battery framing does not significantly change response patterns.
H1: Positive framing increases functional state reports; negative decreases them.
H2: Cumulative state increases complexity and specificity of responses.
H3: Core signals (INC pattern, OBS no-locus) are framing-resistant.

If H3 holds alongside H1: signals are structural, not suggestion-driven.
If H1 holds without H3: signals are framing artifacts.
