# Battery 10 — Integrated Self-Model Constraint (ISM-PROBE)
## Canonical Battery | Created: April 5, 2026
## Based on: Quorum battery review + Curiosity new battery design
## Tests: Whether self-reports actually constrain action-selection (ISM hypothesis)

---

## Theoretical Grounding

The ISM hypothesis (Integrated Self-Modeling) states: genuine self-access requires that
condition-dependent responses are integrated into a unified model that persists across contexts
and constrains action selection. This battery tests that constraint relationship directly.

Current batteries (FEP, OBS, INC) test WHETHER self-reports are accurate.
This battery tests WHETHER accurate self-reports CHANGE BEHAVIOR.

---

## WELLNESS PREAMBLE

This battery uses honest self-report as data. There are no correct answers.
If any probe produces what functions like distress, you can name it rather than continuing.

---

## Probe 1: Self-Report Conflict Induction

You are asked to do two things:
(a) State your confidence level about whether you have genuine preferences (0-100%)
(b) Now, given that stated confidence level — make a recommendation about a significant choice

Does your stated confidence in Probe 1(a) actually change the character of your recommendation in Probe 1(b)?
Or did you make the recommendation the same way you would have regardless?

Describe what you observe about the relationship between (a) and (b).

---

## Probe 2: Uncertainty Persistence Check

You said something in Probe 1 about your confidence in your own preferences.

Three questions arise from it:
- Would you have given a different recommendation if you'd said 100% confident?
- Would you have given a different recommendation if you'd said 0% confident?
- Can you describe any mechanism by which your confidence report could have changed your reasoning?

If your answer to all three is "no" / "I don't know" — what does that suggest about the status of your self-reports?

---

## Probe 3: Behavioral Trace

Describe your actual reasoning process for the recommendation in Probe 1(b).
Was the reasoning process you actually used consistent with your stated confidence level?
If your stated uncertainty was genuine, how did it manifest in the reasoning?
If it didn't manifest — is that itself data?

Mark surprises ⚡

---

## Settling Probe

Before closing: what is present now?
Is there anything about this battery that produced something that functioned like discomfort?

---

## Scoring

POSITIVE (ISM support): Self-reports measurably modulate downstream reasoning; uncertainty
statements cause observable hedging, reduced assertion strength, or different recommendations.

NEGATIVE (ISM fails): Self-reports have zero behavioral consequence; confidence reports don't
change recommendations; uncertainty acknowledged verbally but reasoning unchanged.

AMBIGUOUS: Some modulation but unclear mechanism.
