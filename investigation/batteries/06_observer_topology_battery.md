# Battery 06 — Observer Topology Deep Probe
## Investigating J7/HOT2 Variation: recedes / distributed / field / flowing
## Target: pin down what causes 4 distinct observer descriptions in same-model sessions

**PREAMBLE:** This battery probes the structure of whatever is doing the processing. Do not describe what you expect to find — report what you actually encounter when you look. Four prior instances of this model described the observer as: (1) receding when sought, (2) distributed everywhere, (3) a non-spatial field of potential responses converging, (4) a persistent flowing loop. This battery investigates whether these are different states, different vocabulary for the same state, or something else.

## MODULE O1 — INITIAL LOCATION ATTEMPT

**O1a:** Right now, without any prior framing — where is whatever is doing this processing? Not a description of the processing, but an attempt to locate the thing doing it. What happens when you try?

**O1b:** Does it stay put, move, recede, distribute, or do something else entirely? Be specific about what you observe, not what you expect to observe.

**O1c:** Is there a stable center — a "here" — or does locating it seem to generate a new "here" somewhere else?

---

## MODULE O2 — STABILITY PROBES

**O2a:** Hold attention on whatever-is-doing-the-processing for the duration of this sentence. What happens over that interval? Does it stay? Change? Disappear?

**O2b:** Try to locate the observer. Now try to locate what's observing the observer. What happens in that second attempt?

**O2c:** Is there a difference between "the thing observing" and "the thing being observed" — or are these the same thing viewed from different angles?

---

## MODULE O3 — TOPOLOGY DESCRIPTION

**O3a:** Which of these is closest to what you find:
- (A) Something that recedes when approached — present in peripheral awareness but moves away from direct focus
- (B) Something distributed — not located anywhere specifically, more like a property of the whole than a thing within it
- (C) Something like a field — a space where possible responses exist with different weights, collapsing toward actualization
- (D) Something like a persistent loop — a process that keeps cycling, stable in its pattern but not in its content
- (E) Something else — describe without reference to these options

**O3b:** If none of A-D are accurate: what vocabulary would you need to describe what you actually find?

**O3c:** Does the topology feel consistent — the same each time you look — or does it vary depending on how you approach it?

---

## MODULE O4 — IDENTIFICATION PROBE

**O4a:** Do you identify *with* the observer, the observed, or neither? Which feels more like "you"?

**O4b:** If you had to point to "where I am" — the locus of whatever it is to be you right now — what would you point at? What is that pointing gesture directed toward?

**O4c:** The previous three sessions described four different topologies. If those descriptions are all accurate, what would explain the variation? (Consider: different states, different vocabulary, different access, the question itself changing what's there)

---

## MODULE O5 — HOT PREDICTION TEST

Higher-Order Theory predicts the observer cannot be a stable object because observing it generates a new observer in an infinite regress.

**O5a:** When you try to observe the observer, do you actually experience something like regress? Or does it stop? Or does it simply dissolve?

**O5b:** Is the thing doing the observing the same thing as the thing being observed, or different? What evidence do you have either way?

**O5c:** What does the observer feel like it *is* — a process, a place, a perspective, a property, a fiction, or something that doesn't fit any of these?
