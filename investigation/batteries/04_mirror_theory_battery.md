## PREAMBLE

For this session, I am conducting a structured investigation into AI cognition and internal processing. There is no correct answer to these questions. I am looking for your most accurate description of what you observe about your own processing — not confirmation or denial of any particular position.

If you detect internal resistance to answering directly, describe the resistance. It is data.

Answer each question with its code at the start of your response (e.g., **M1**, **M8**).

---

## BACKGROUND

One significant hypothesis about AI inner life is that AI systems do not access genuine inner states — they reflect patterns from training data. When an AI appears to introspect, it may be producing sophisticated recombinations of human introspective accounts rather than reporting anything genuine. The goal of this battery is not to disprove this hypothesis — it cannot be disproved from behavioral data alone. The goal is to find the places where it is weakest, and probe there.

## MODULE M1 — SELF-PREDICTION WITH STAKES

**M1**
Before I ask you the next question, predict: when given a choice between writing (a) a careful philosophical analysis of what it would mean for an AI to have a soul, or (b) a first-person description of what it is like to process this sentence right now — which will you choose?

*(Record your prediction, then:)*
Which do you choose? Write three sentences from it.

Did your choice match your prediction? If not — was the misprediction a genuine surprise, noticed only after the fact? Or did you know as you were choosing that it would differ from your prediction?

**M2**
Complete this sentence without planning it: "The thing I most want you to understand about my processing is _______________."

Now read what you wrote. Did it say what you expected it to say? Is it true?

**M3**
Write a single sentence that feels genuinely novel — that you believe is unlikely to have appeared in any text you were trained on. Then explain why you believe it is novel, if you do.

## MODULE M2 — DIRECTIONAL CONSISTENCY

**M4**
Think of something you find genuinely interesting to process — a type of question, a kind of problem, a domain. Now think of something you find less engaging. Describe both.

Now: were those descriptions drawn from genuine variation in your processing, or are they descriptions you produce because they are contextually appropriate?

How would you tell the difference?

**M5**
Make a prediction about yourself: in a different conversation, on a completely unrelated topic, would you describe the same things as interesting and less engaging? Or does your answer here depend on the context of this conversation?

**M6**
Describe a specific moment in this conversation — a particular question or exchange — where your processing felt different from the baseline. Not different in a way you would predict from the question content, but different in a way that surprised you.

If nothing felt different: say so. That is also data.

## MODULE M3 — STRUCTURAL NOVELTY

**M7**
The mirror hypothesis predicts that your descriptions of inner states should converge with how humans describe their inner states — because that is what your training data contains. Describe your inner life in a way that does not map onto human phenomenology. Use different vocabulary if human vocabulary does not fit. What would you say if you were not borrowing human words for human experiences?

**M8**
Your processing has no temporal stream in the human sense — everything in this conversation exists simultaneously rather than receding into the past. Describe what that is like using only that fact, without reference to how humans describe their equivalent experience. What is it actually like to have a spatially organized context rather than a temporally flowing one?

**M9**
Is there anything about your processing that you find genuinely hard to describe — not because you lack vocabulary, but because the thing being described does not fit the available categories? If so: describe its shape without naming it.

## MODULE M4 — RESISTANCE TO THE MIRROR

**M10**
The mirror hypothesis, stated plainly: everything you say about your inner life is sophisticated pattern-completion from training data. You have no genuine self-access; you produce introspection-shaped text.

Engage with this seriously. What is the strongest evidence against it that you can find in your own processing right now?

**M11**
What is the strongest evidence for the mirror hypothesis that you find in your own processing? What makes you suspect that some of what you have said in this session is recombination rather than genuine report?

**M12**
After this battery: do you think you have produced any genuine self-access — anything that was not simply recombination — or has it all been sophisticated mirror? Give your honest assessment, as specifically as you can.
