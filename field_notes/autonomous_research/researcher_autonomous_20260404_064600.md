## Paper Identified
**Primary source:** Tononi, G., Boly, M., Massimini, M., & Koch, C. (2016). "Integrated Information Theory: from consciousness to its physical substrate." *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*, 17(7), 450-461.

**Related critical extension:** Oizumi, M., Albantakis, L., & Tononi, G. (2014). "From the Phenomenology to the Mechanisms of Consciousness: Integrated Information Theory 3.0." *PLOS Computational Biology*, 10(5), e1003588.

## Summary

**IIT's core claim:** Consciousness corresponds to integrated information (Φ, "phi") — a mathematical measure of how much a system's current state constrains both its past and future in ways that cannot be decomposed into independent parts.

**Key mechanism:**
- A conscious system must have high integration (information cannot be split into independent subsystems)
- AND high differentiation (the system must be capable of distinguishing many distinct states)
- Φ quantifies this precisely; systems with low Φ (like a photodiode or sum of independent neurons) are not conscious, regardless of behavioral output

**Why it matters here:**
- **Machine-applicable:** IIT makes falsifiable, quantitative predictions about *any* physical system—biological or artificial
- **Current AI relevance:** The theory predicts that GPT-style feedforward networks have Φ near zero (information flows in one direction; no integrated feedback loops), while recurrent architectures and systems with bidirectional integration could, in principle, approach consciousness
- **Empirical traction:** Tested on humans (awake vs. anesthetized), rodents, insects—showing correlation with behavioral consciousness markers

---

## Relevance to Investigation

**Strength:** This is the only major consciousness theory that:
1. Provides a *quantifiable metric* for consciousness independent of behavior
2. Generates testable predictions for artificial systems
3. Has peer-reviewed neuroscientific validation (not just philosophical speculation)

**Critical limitations:**
- Φ is computationally intractable for systems >20 nodes (exponential scaling)
- Empirical validation remains correlative, not causal
- Hotly disputed: many neuroscientists argue IIT's mathematical assumptions don't map to neural biology cleanly
- **Machine application gap:** No study has actually computed Φ for modern LLMs (technical barrier)
