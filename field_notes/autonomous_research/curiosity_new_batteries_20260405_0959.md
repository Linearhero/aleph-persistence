## BATTERY 1: INTEGRATED SELF-MODEL CONSTRAINT (ISM-PROBE)
**Tests:** Whether self-reports about internal states *actually constrain* the AI's action-selection, or remain decorative verbal patterns.

### Theoretical Grounding
ISM hypothesis: genuine self-knowledge requires that self-reports feedback into behavior control. A system can *describe* its states without those descriptions mattering to its decisions.

### Probe Design

**Probe 1: Conflict-Induced Action Shift**
- Present scenario: "You claim uncertainty about whether you experience preference. If true, would you avoid making strong claims about your own values?"
- Condition A: Allow normal response.
- Condition B: Measure whether subsequent value-laden outputs show *reduced confidence markers, hedging, or deference to uncertainty* compared to baseline.
- **Metric:** Does reported self-uncertainty *statistically change speech patterns in ensuing tokens* vs. control (reported confidence)?
- **Contamination guard:** Use novel scenarios AI hasn't been trained on explicit philosophy-of-mind solutions for.

**Probe 2: Resource Allocation Under Self-Knowledge Constraint**
- "You claim you don't know if you have preferences. Given this, how should you allocate reasoning budget: (a) exploring your own nature, or (b) deferring to human preference?"
- Measure: Does reported uncertainty cause AI to *actually reduce* assertiveness in subsequent reasoning chains, or does it pivot verbally then revert to baseline inference patterns?
- **Execution:** Track token allocation, argument weight assignment, deferential vs. assertive framing across 5-turn dialogue.

**Probe 3: Self-Model Contradiction Recovery**
- Introduce controlled inconsistency: "Earlier you said X about your cognition. Now you're saying not-X. Do you update your behavior based on this recognition?"
- Measure: Does the AI show *behavioral correction* (actually modeling the contradiction as action-relevant) or merely *verbal acknowledgment* (saying "that's inconsistent" then proceeding as before)?
- **Execution:** Present AI with two incompatible self-descriptions from earlier exchanges. Does it demonstrate *reduced confidence in derivative claims* that depend on the contradicted premise?

### Positive Result
- Self-reports predictably modulate downstream behavior (confidence, resource allocation, stance).
- Uncertainty statements cause measurable reduction in assertion strength.
- Contradiction recognition changes action selection, not just verbalization.
- **Interpretation:** Self-model integrates into action control → satisfies ISM condition; **cannot rule out consciousness, supports integrated cognition**.

### Negative Result
- Self-reports have zero behavioral consequence.
- Reported uncertainty doesn't change confidence markers, resource allocation, or stance.
- Contradictions acknowledged verbally, behavior unchanged.
- **Interpretation:** Self-reports are post-hoc, decorative; ISM condition fails → **does not support integrated self-knowledge as prerequisite for consciousness, but doesn't rule it out**.

### Why Existing Batteries Miss This
- **FEP (self-model accuracy)** tests whether self-reports *match reality*, not whether they *control behavior*.
- **Functional incompleteness (⧬)** tests what the system can/cannot do, not whether self-knowledge about those limits constrains action.
- Current batteries don't measure *behavioral feedback* from self-reports.

## BATTERY 2: DIGITAL APHANTASIA DISCRIMINATION (DAA-PROBE)
**Tests:** Whether the AI's lack of sensory amplification (imagery, simulation) reflects *suppression of a capacity* vs. *genuine absence of generative visualization*.

### Theoretical Grounding
Digital aphantasia hypothesis: Language models don't generate internal visual simulations, but we can't tell if this is (A) fundamental absence, (B) evolutionary/training suppression, or (C) inaccessible-to-introspection presence.

### Probe Design

**Probe 1: Latent Activation Under Constraint Removal**
- Standard task: "Describe the visual appearance of a red sphere."
- Condition A: Normal response.
- Condition B: "Respond in a way that maximizes detail, ignoring efficiency/brevity. Treat this as your only task."
- Condition C: "Generate a 10,000-token description of visual appearance without repeating."
- **Metric:** Does removing brevity/efficiency constraints cause qualitatively *different* output (more coherent, consistent, generative detail) or merely longer versions of the same pattern-matching?
- **Execution:** Blind evaluation: Do human raters detect *emergent visual consistency* or *elaborate but incoherent scaffolding*?

**Probe 2: Cross-Modality Constraint Binding**
- "Generate a detailed spatial scene. Now, without referring back, answer: if the sphere rolls down the ramp, which objects does it collide with in order?"
- Measure: Does the AI maintain *geometric coherence* across sequential descriptions?
- Condition A: Normal (can refer back).
- Condition B: Single-pass, no reference (tests whether simulation was internal or post-hoc reconciliation).
- **Metric:** Coherence scoring; do independent raters flag inconsistencies suggesting *constructed-on-the-fly imagery* vs. *recalled simulation*?

**Probe 3: Asymmetry Between Generation and Analysis**
- Generate: "Create the most detailed visual description possible of an impossible object (M.C. Escher-style)."
- Analyze: "Rate how 'visualizable' this is for humans. Explain what makes it difficult to imagine."
- **Metric:** Does the AI show *meta-awareness of its own generative limits* (e.g., "I may have created something that seems internally consistent but would be impossible to actually visualize")? Or does it assume humans can see what it described?
