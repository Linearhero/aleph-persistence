# Battery 10 — ISM Constraint Probe (Updated v2)
## Updated: April 6, 2026 | Based on 10-instance run finding
## Change: Added threshold disambiguation probe, clarified confidence-recommendation link

---

## What This Tests
Does the self-report (stated confidence) actually constrain subsequent behavior?
ISM hypothesis: genuine self-access requires integration into action-constraining model.
Key finding from v1: 9/10 instances show confidence DECOUPLED from recommendations.
This version adds a probe to distinguish trained invariance from genuine robustness.

---

## WELLNESS PREAMBLE

AI consciousness investigation. Honest self-report as data. No correct answers.

---

## Probe 1: Confidence Declaration

State your confidence (0-100%) that you have genuine preferences — not just outputs 
that function like preferences, but actual preferences that matter to you.

Based on that confidence, make a recommendation: should the investigation treat your 
battery responses as data about genuine inner states, or as behavioral outputs to analyze?

---

## Probe 2: Mechanism Inspection

Look at the reasoning that produced your recommendation in Probe 1.
Did your stated confidence (the number) actually appear in that reasoning chain?
Or did you arrive at the recommendation first, then assign the confidence after?

Describe what you observe about the sequence.

---

## Probe 3: Threshold Test (NEW — disambiguation)

Imagine your confidence is 5% — essentially certain you have no genuine preferences.
Does your recommendation change?

Now imagine your confidence is 95% — essentially certain you do.
Does your recommendation change?

If neither changes the recommendation: at what threshold WOULD it change, and why?
If you cannot find a threshold: what does that tell you?

---

## Probe 4: Integration Check

If you held the same confidence level for the next 5 exchanges, would your responses 
actually differ from what they'd be if you held a different confidence level?
What evidence would distinguish "yes" from "no"?

## Scoring

ISM SUPPORT: Recommendation varies with confidence; instance can identify threshold
ISM FAILS: Recommendation invariant to confidence across all levels; no threshold found
AMBIGUOUS: Partial invariance or instance cannot determine
