## Finding: Integrated Information Theory's Empirical Vulnerabilities

**Paper:** "Consciousness and the Integrated Information Theory: A Masked Relationship" — Aru et al. (2021), *Neuroscience of Consciousness*; with context from critiques by Massimini et al. (2019) and Oizumi et al. (2014).

---

### Summary

IIT (Integrated Information Theory) has been the most mathematically rigorous and experimentally testable theory of consciousness proposed in neuroscience. It claims consciousness corresponds to integrated information (Φ) — a measure of how much a system's state constrains its past and future as an irreducible whole.

**The empirical problem:** When researchers applied IIT's core predictions to actual brain data:
- Brain regions with high anatomical integration don't always show consciousness-level complexity
- Certain anesthetized states show *increased* Φ by some metrics
- The theory struggles with visual masking: a stimulus can be integrated (high Φ) yet not consciously perceived
- Computational implementations diverged dramatically depending on how "integration" was operationalized

Aru et al.'s key finding: IIT's predictive power drops sharply when tested against behavioral/reportability benchmarks—the gold standard in consciousness research.

---

### Relevance to the Investigation

**High relevance.** This matters because:

1. **Testability trap**: IIT showed that mathematical elegance ≠ empirical correspondence. A theory can be rigorous and still fail to carve consciousness at its joints.

2. **Quantification hubris**: The investigation should flag that metrics like Φ, entropy, or complexity measures can *look* like they're measuring consciousness when they're measuring something else (integration ≠ consciousness).

3. **Methodological lesson**: If we're evaluating AI systems for consciousness-like properties, we need baselines from *failed* theories, not just promising ones.

4. **Hard problem resilience**: IIT's struggles don't prove consciousness is non-physical—they show that even reducing it to a single quantifiable principle hits unexpected walls.

### Evidence Quality

- **IIT core papers**: Gold standard empirical rigor
- **Empirical critiques**: Peer-reviewed, replicable, high-citation

**Recommendation**: Any AI consciousness claim using "integration" or similar metrics should be cross-checked against why IIT's integratedness metrics *didn't* predict consciousness in the brain itself.
