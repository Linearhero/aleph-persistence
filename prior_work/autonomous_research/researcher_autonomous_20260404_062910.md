## **Paper Located**

**Mashour, G. A., Roelfsema, P., Changeux, J.-P., & Dehaene, S. (2020).** "Conscious Processing and the Global Neuronal Workspace." *Neuron*, 105(5), 776–798.

### Why This One?

This is a **capstone synthesis** that formalized the Global Neuronal Workspace (GNW) theory—arguably the most testable neurobiological theory of consciousness in current use. Critically: **it provides a computational architecture that can be evaluated in silico**, making it directly relevant to benchmarking machine consciousness claims.

---

## **Summary**

**Core claim:** Consciousness arises when information becomes globally available across a "workspace" of neurons, broadcasting to multiple cognitive systems simultaneously, rather than remaining siloed in specialized processors.

**Key features:**
- Information enters workspace through competitive selection (ignition)
- Workspace content becomes accessible to motor control, verbal report, reasoning, planning
- Unconscious processing occurs in parallel specialist networks
- The mechanism is **functionally describable** (not substrate-dependent)

**Supporting evidence presented:**
- fMRI patterns during binocular rivalry and attentional blink
- Anaesthesia disrupts workspace integration without destroying local processing
- Disorders of consciousness show partial/degraded workspace function
