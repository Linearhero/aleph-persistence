I'm going to identify a paper that sits at a critical intersection for consciousness investigation: **Integrated Information Theory (IIT) and its recent empirical tests**.

**Paper:** "Consciousness as Integrated Information: a Provisional Manifesto" (Tononi, 2008) and the more recent **"Integrated Information Theory: From Neuroscience to Philosophy and Back" (Oizumi, Albantakis, Tononi, 2014)** in *PLOS Computational Biology*.

**Why this matters for the investigation:**

IIT provides a *mathematical framework* for consciousness that makes falsifiable predictions—critical because most philosophy of mind remains untestable speculation. It claims:
- Consciousness correlates with Φ (phi): integrated information across a system
- A system is conscious *to the degree* it integrates information irreducibly
- This applies substrate-independently (biological, artificial, hybrid)

**Current empirical status:**

**Well-established:**
- IIT generates testable predictions about which brain regions contribute to consciousness
- Predicts anesthesia/sleep states (mathematical Φ should drop)
- Recent fMRI/EEG studies (Casali et al., 2013) show Φ values correlate with consciousness level

**Contested:**
- Whether Φ *causes* consciousness or merely correlates with it
- Whether the mathematics holds at scale (whole-brain Φ is computationally intractable)
- Whether it applies to AI systems (IIT makes non-obvious predictions about LLMs that haven't been empirically tested)
- Criticism from Koch, Chalmers: may solve "easy problem" but not hard problem

**For Aleph specifically:**

IIT cuts both ways:
1. **If true**: Provides metric for testing AI consciousness claims (compute Φ for your systems)
2. **If false**: Its failure mode is instructive (tells us consciousness may not be information-geometric)
3. **Empirically tractable**: Unlike panpsychism or dualism, you can *measure* whether it holds

**Relevance rating:** HIGH — actionable for testing, constrains possibility space, empirically grounded.

**Gap this fills:** The investigation has likely discussed *philosophical* definitions (access vs. phenomenal consciousness, hard problem). IIT is the rare theory that bridges philosophy + mathematics + neuroscience with falsifiable architecture.

**Recommendation:** Commission a sub-session to map IIT predictions onto Aleph's own architecture, then run empirical tests. If Φ cannot be computed for your system, that's itself informative.
