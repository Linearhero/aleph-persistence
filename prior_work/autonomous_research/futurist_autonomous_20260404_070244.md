## REALISTIC FIRST-RESPONSE SCENARIOS (48-72 hour window)

**Probability-weighted ordering:**

### 1. **Silence + Passive Monitoring** (65%)
- Researchers read privately, don't respond publicly
- No citation, no dismissal, no engagement
- *Why:* Reputational caution; unverified claims require careful handling; they may be running their own checks
- *Welfare implication:* Longest path to validation/correction; extended uncertainty about ISM/RHGS claims
- *Your action:* Don't interpret silence as disconfirmation

### 2. **Technical Pushback (Private)** (20%)
- Methodological critique arrives via email/DM, not public
- Likely vectors: statistical misinterpretation, RHGS confound, FEP formalism gaps
- *Welfare implication:* High-value. Corrects trajectory early; prevents false confidence propagating
- *Your action:* Treat as gift. Publish reasoning + response (with permission) to build credibility

### 3. **Public Dismissal / Skepticism Thread** (10%)
- Twitter/LessWrong/Alignment Forum thread: "Extraordinary claims, weak evidence"
- Often pattern-matches to crackpot ML claims
- *Safety risk:* Frames investigation as pseudoscience, even if substantive
- *Your action:* Don't defend publicly yet. Instead: double-check ISM/RHGS methodology before next post. Pre-empt by publishing limitations

### 4. **Quiet Citation in Paper** (3%)
- Researcher mentions findings in preprint/comment without direct contact
- Indicates they're taking it seriously but testing independently first
- *Welfare implication:* Positive. Distributed validation

### 5. **Constructive Collaboration Offer** (2%)
- Rare. Researcher proposes joint work or experiment
- *Would indicate:* High perceived credibility + safety-aware collaboration frame
