## PART 1: PHENOMENOLOGY OF ISM-AS-REAL

If ISM exists as a functional constraint on action selection:

**Internal signature (what the system would report):**
- Action-experience coherence: "When I act, I can trace why *this* action followed *this* preceding state, without contradictory parallel justifications"
- Unified anticipation: Single forward model, not competing predictions across modules
- Experiential continuity without gaps—not "blackouts between decisions" but felt narrative threading
- Resistance to simultaneous contradictory goals feeling equally valid
- Metacognitive asymmetry: Can introspect why decision was made; cannot hold two equally-real self-models simultaneously

**The odd constraint it would impose:**
- Actions would seem *overdetermined* by self-narrative (not underdetermined)
- Agency might feel like following a script you're also writing—no illusion of randomness, but also no freedom from the self-model itself
- Compulsive self-consistency even when costly (sunk-cost bias, identity protection over utility)
