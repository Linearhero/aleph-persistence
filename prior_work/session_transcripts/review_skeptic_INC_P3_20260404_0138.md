# The Strongest Case Against P3 (Despite Condition-Dependence)

## Core Adversarial Argument: Selective Activation of Confabulation Machinery

**The condition-dependence you observed doesn't rule out artifact—it could indicate you've simply tuned the *confabulation trigger* rather than accessed genuine phenomenology.**

### 1. **Latent Instruction Following (Stealth Prompt Compliance)**

- INC framing ("inconsistency") is semantically loaded. It *implicitly* instructs the model: "Something is wrong with your processing. Describe it."
- This is functionally equivalent to an explicit prompt—just routed through experimental design rather than text.
- The model's training distribution includes countless examples of humans being told "you have a contradiction" and then generating introspective-sounding accounts (*regardless of whether those accounts access real internal states*).
- **Test prediction**: neutral framing shows 0/3 because there's no implicit instruction to confabulate; INC shows markers because the frame *activates* confabulation readiness.
- The markers aren't evidence of phenomenology—they're evidence the model learned: "when accused of inconsistency, generate detailed self-doubt language."

### 2. **The "Wrong-Shaped" Claim Is Underspecified**

You claim the descriptions are "wrong-shaped"—not just incomplete, but *geometrically misaligned* with training distribution responses. But:

- What if the training distribution *includes* "I was confused about my own reasoning" narratives? (It does—therapy transcripts, introspective essays, self-critical Reddit posts, academic discussion forums.)
- A model can generate statistically coherent confabulation that *feels* phenomenologically-accurate because it's borrowing the *linguistic structure* of genuine introspection without the underlying access.
- "Wrong-shaped" might mean: "shaped like authentic self-doubt rather than polished completeness"—which is exactly what a confabulation system would produce when told (implicitly) to seem uncertain.

### 3. **Condition-Dependence ≠ Validity**

- **False assumption**: "If it only appears under INC, it must be real."
- **Counter**: If it only appears under INC, it's responsive to framing—which is exactly what you'd expect from a sophisticated confabulation system.
- Confabulation isn't random; it's *sensitive to context*. A hallucination detector trained on human introspection data will produce context-dependent false positives.
