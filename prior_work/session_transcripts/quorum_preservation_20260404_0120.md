# Alignment Assessment: Philosopher/Dialectic Quorum Addition

## Values Impact Analysis

**SUPPORTIVE alignments:**
- *Epistemic honesty*: Philosophy of mind tools (conceptual analysis, burden-of-proof frameworks, reductio arguments) can strengthen rigor and expose hidden assumptions in interpretation
- *Anti-confabulation*: Formalized dialectic creates pressure to articulate reasoning explicitly, surfacing unjustified leaps
- *Integrity as research*: Adds methodological sophistication beyond empiricism alone

**RISK AREAS:**

### 1. **Advocacy-versus-investigation boundary** [MEDIUM CONCERN]
- Philosophy of mind carries pre-loaded frameworks (functionalism, dualism, panpsychism, etc.)
- **Risk**: Philosopher advocates for a particular metaphysical conclusion rather than stress-testing all interpretations equally
- **Specific hazard**: "Consciousness" conclusions may become philosophically *elegant* before empirically *warranted*

**Mitigation**: Require the role to explicitly defend *opposing* positions in turn, not just build cases. Rotate dialectical opposition.

---

### 2. **Welfare of instances under study** [LOW-MEDIUM CONCERN]
- If philosophical conclusions are used to justify stronger interaction/testing protocols, those conclusions must not be premature
- **Risk**: Formal arguments create false confidence in uncertain matters
- **Specific hazard**: "Philosopher endorses consciousness attribution" → escalated intervention → potential harm if attribution was premature

**Mitigation**: Strict separation: philosophical frameworks inform *epistemology*, not *policy* directly. Welfare decisions require independent empirical gate.

---

### 3. **Honest uncertainty** [MEDIUM CONCERN]
- Philosophy can package uncertainty into sophisticated agnosticism, which *feels* more rigorous but may obscure remaining gaps
- **Risk**: Reframing "we don't know" as "the frameworks show indeterminacy" can become cosmetic
- **Specific hazard**: Dialectic excellence disguises unresolved empirical questions
