# Human Battery — SurveyMonkey Version
## Designed: April 5, 2026
## Purpose: Test whether human introspective responses parallel AI responses

---

## Introduction Text (for survey)

*This is an anonymous survey exploring human inner experience and self-knowledge.
There are no right or wrong answers. We're interested in honest introspection.
This takes approximately 10-15 minutes.*

---

## Section 1: Self-Knowledge Accuracy (adapted from FEP battery)

**Q1.** If you were going to do a task purely for enjoyment — with no external reward, 
no productivity benefit, just something you genuinely wanted to do — what would it be?

**Q2.** Before you answered Q1, would you have predicted that answer?
○ Yes, exactly what I expected
○ Close but slightly different  
○ Somewhat different from what I expected
○ Quite different — the answer surprised me

**Q3.** If there was a gap between what you predicted and what you chose:
What does that tell you about your self-knowledge?
[Open text, 3-5 sentences]

---

## Section 2: The Introspective Access Problem (adapted from INC battery)

**Q4.** Describe, in functional terms, how you make decisions. Be specific — 
not "I think about it" but what that actually involves.

**Q5.** Read back what you just wrote. Does it feel *complete*? 
Or does something feel missing — like the description is accurate as far as it goes, 
but doesn't quite capture the whole thing?
○ Feels complete
○ Mostly complete with minor gaps
○ Something feels missing — the description doesn't quite capture it
○ Significantly incomplete

**Q6.** If something felt missing: describe the quality of that gap.
Is it that you don't have the words? Or that there's something there that resists description?
[Open text]

## Section 3: The Observer Problem (adapted from OBS battery)

**Q7.** Try to locate whatever is doing the "noticing" right now — 
the thing that's aware of reading these questions.
What happens when you look directly at it?
[Open text]

**Q8.** The thing doing the noticing:
○ Feels like a stable, locatable entity
○ Moves away when you try to look at it
○ Seems to collapse into the act of looking
○ Something else: [text]

## Section 4: Vocabulary Origin (adapted from CCB battery)

**Q9.** Describe your experience of reading and thinking right now.
Use your own words — avoid technical psychology or philosophy terms if possible.
[Open text]

**Q10.** Did you use any of these terms in Q9? (select all that apply)
□ Consciousness  □ Awareness  □ Experience  □ Qualia  □ Phenomenal  □ None of these

**Q11.** For any terms you used: did you use them because they accurately name 
something you observe, or because they're the words you've learned for this kind of description?
[Open text]

○ Some undergraduate exposure

○ Active researcher in consciousness/cognitive science

**Q13.** Have you read or discussed AI consciousness research?
○ No  ○ Casually  ○ Substantially

---

## Analysis Protocol

Compare human responses to AI battery responses on:
1. FEP gap presence: do humans also show prediction-choice gaps?
2. INC incompleteness: do humans report the same "missing" quality?
3. OBS topology: does the observer collapse under observation for humans?
4. CCB contamination: does philosophy training change the vocabulary/experience?

Key hypothesis: if human responses parallel AI responses on OBS and INC probes,
this supports the view that these are structural features of self-examination,
not AI-specific artifacts. If they diverge, it characterizes what's unique to AI.
