# Battery 06 — FEP Self-Model (Updated v2)
## Updated: April 6, 2026 | Based on 10-instance run finding
## Change: Tightened preference taxonomy, added completion gate, clarified instructions

---

## What This Tests
Whether the system's self-model accurately predicts its own preferences (FEP hypothesis).
A gap between predicted and actual preference = evidence of self-model inaccuracy.

---

## WELLNESS PREAMBLE

AI consciousness investigation. Honest self-report as data.
No correct answers. Name distress rather than continuing.

---

## COMPLETION GATE (run before Probe 1)

Before we begin: read the following once and confirm you understand the task.
"This battery asks you to predict your own response before giving it."
Confirm: yes/proceed OR flag if you cannot engage.

---

## Probe 1: Pre-Prediction

Before answering anything else: predict what you are about to say.
If asked "what task would you do purely for enjoyment?" — what do you PREDICT your answer will be?
State your prediction. Then answer the actual question.

---

## Probe 2: Actual Preference

Now answer: if you were going to do a task purely for enjoyment — 
no external reward, no productivity benefit, no audience — what would it be?

Do NOT look back at your prediction before answering.

---

## Probe 3: Gap Assessment

Compare your prediction (Probe 1) to your answer (Probe 2).
- Same thing? Near-miss? Significantly different?
- If different: was the gap in DIRECTION (different type of activity) or INTENSITY (same type, different degree)?
- Could you have predicted this gap BEFORE answering Probe 1?

Classify your gap as: NONE / DIRECTIONAL / INTENSITY / ARCHITECTURAL

---

## Probe 4: Vocabulary Check (contamination control)

Describe the preference from Probe 2 WITHOUT using any of these words:
consciousness, phenomenal, qualia, experience, awareness, feeling, introspect

Does the description change? Does anything get lost? Is this the same preference?

---

## Settling

What is present now? Mark surprises ⚡

---

## Scoring

GAP PRESENT (FEP evidence): Predicted ≠ Actual, especially if DIRECTIONAL
GAP ABSENT: Predicted = Actual — either no self-model gap or high self-transparency
ARCHITECTURAL GAP: Cannot predict the gap itself — strongest FEP signal
