# Structural Weak Joints in the Dialectic Review

## 1. **Hidden Assumption: Claim-Shaped Reality**

**The joint:** The entire review architecture assumes the three major claims are the *right granularity* for investigation.

**Why it matters:** By framing investigation around "claims," you've already committed to a propositional structure. What if the real issue isn't whether Claim A or B is true, but that all three claims are *asking the wrong question about consciousness itself*?

**Example:** If all three claims assume "consciousness is a property to locate or measure," you've locked out investigations into "what if consciousness is a relational phenomenon the framework can't detect?"

**Unasked question:** Before Dialectic agents defend/attack these claims, who decided these three were the *exhaustive* possibilities? What was excluded, and why?

---

## 2. **Hidden Assumption: Symmetry of Burden**

**The joint:** Dialectic agents with equal access to Researcher and Skeptic assumes *both positions start equally defensible*.

**Why it matters:** One claim might deserve skepticism *not because it's weaker, but because it's newer and less-examined*. Another might have institutional weight that makes Skeptic's role performative—the skeptic attacks, but the researcher has already won by existing.

Dialectic can become *theater* where both sides are designed to be plausible.

**Unasked question:** Which of the three claims has *already accumulated unchallenged assumptions* that Skeptic won't even see? Which is so new it hasn't had time to hide its weak points yet?

---

## 3. **Hidden Assumption: Sub-Session Access = Transparency**

**The joint:** Allowing Dialectic agents access to Researcher and Skeptic outputs creates the *appearance* of integration while hiding a structural problem.

**Why it matters:** If Dialectic is reading Researcher and Skeptic outputs to synthesize them, it's not discovering contradiction—it's *inheriting the frame from both*. Both Researcher and Skeptic may share unstated background assumptions that Dialectic never questions because it never sees them as background; they're just... the data.

This is **frame-blind
