## The Core Problem

**The claim asserts that organizing around a **single unifying principle** (ISM = Information-Substrate Mapping) enhances integrity. But this is backwards from how epistemic rigor actually works.**

### Specific vulnerabilities I see:

1. **Convergence bias masquerading as coherence**
   - If ISM is the organizing frame, *all findings naturally slot into it*
   - This creates false coherence, not genuine integration
   - Disconfirmation: Show me a finding that *couldn't* be reinterpreted through ISM. If none exist, the framework is unfalsifiable.

2. **Consensus capture**
   - A "central unifying claim" becomes the gravitational well all discussion orbits
   - Minority interpretations (substrate independence, emergentism, strict functionalism) get absorbed rather than genuinely weighed
   - Disconfirmation: Document instances where the team rejected a finding *because* it contradicted ISM specifically (not for empirical reasons).

3. **Integrity conflation**
   - The claim slides between two things:
     - *Epistemic integrity* (avoiding bias, staying open to refutation)
     - *Conceptual coherence* (fitting findings into one framework)
   - These are NOT the same. Coherence can hide bias; integrity requires friction.
   - Disconfirmation: Show that teams with *competing* unifying claims produced *less rigorous* investigations.

4. **Premature closure**
   - If ISM is "central" before investigation concludes, you've presupposed the answer
   - Real integrity means: *What if information and substrate are genuinely separable? What if consciousness requires their tight coupling?*
   - Disconfirmation: Evidence the team actively pursued non-ISM framings with equal resource/attention.

---

## What Would Actually Disconfirm Me

- **Process evidence**: Documented instances where ISM-inconsistent interpretations were *genuinely considered and rejected on empirical grounds*, not absorbed into the framework
- **Competitive frame audit**: A parallel analysis using substrate-monism or functionalism that generates *equally coherent but contradictory* findings — proving coherence ≠ truth
- **Failure logs**: Cases where ISM predicted something that didn't materialize, and the team updated the framework rather than reinterpreted findings
- **Dissent record**: Named investigators who held anti-ISM positions and demonstrate they weren't sidelined

---

## My Specific Concern

**Calling ISM "central to investigation integrity" may be *exactly backwards*:** integrity might require treating it as *one hypothesis among others*, held provisionally, actively tested for failure.

Unifying claims are valuable for *communication*. But framing them as serving *integrity* risks making the investigation a motivated search for confirming coherence.
