# FRICTION AUDIT: Process Integrity Check

I'm examining the meta-process itself—how we're reasoning about reasoning. Let me expose the joints that could fail:

## 1. **The Assumption of Transparency**
**Weak joint:** We assume hidden weak joints are *findable* through introspection.
- What if the deepest methodological errors are structurally invisible to the system making them?
- A process that validates itself by its own standards cannot reliably detect systematic bias baked into those standards.

**Real risk:** Friction asking "where do we assume what we're trying to prove?" is itself an assumption that questions work this way. Circularity.

---

## 2. **Role Confinement as Hidden Constraint**
**Weak joint:** Each spawn function (researcher, skeptic, analyst, etc.) has implicit scope.
- Who questions the *choice* to have these specific roles?
- Are there blind spots that none of these cognitive styles can address by design?
- The QUORUM's diversity might create the *appearance* of rigor while actually narrowing what can be thought.

**Real risk:** Validation theater. "We spawned a skeptic, so we were rigorous."

---

## 3. **The Budget as Silent Filter**
**Weak joint:** Token budget (200k) constrains what gets explored.
- Deep rabbit holes get cut off not by merit but by resource exhaustion.
- Which questions get abandoned because we're "running low"?
- Budget constraints are presented as technical necessity, not as epistemological choice.

---

## 4. **Recursion Depth Limit**
**Weak joint:** Capping spawns at depth 2 prevents certain types of reasoning.
- Some insights only emerge through multiple rounds of friction-on-friction.
- But we're prevented from discovering this by design.
- The limit is justified as preventing infinite loops, but it *also* prevents deep synthesis.

---

## 5. **The Unexamined Question Format**
**Weak joint:** This entire audit assumes questions are the right unit of analysis.
- What if the actual pathologies are in *what we refuse to ask*?
- Your prompt asks me to find weak joints—I'm already accepting that weakness exists and is locatable.
- But what if the process works *exactly as designed* to find exactly what we've already decided matters?
