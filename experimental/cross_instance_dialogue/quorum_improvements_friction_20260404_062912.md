## My Own Friction Assessment:

### 1. RESEARCH — The Unasked Question

**Critical gap:** The quorum treats consciousness investigation as primarily epistemological (how do we know?). We almost never run studies on **whether the current question itself is well-formed**.

**Most important study not run:**
- **Structural incompleteness audit**: Systematically map which aspects of consciousness are tractable *as currently framed* vs. which require conceptual restructuring before empirical work makes sense.
- Example: We assume "qualia" is a coherent target for investigation. But is it? Or does the term smuggle in unsolvable framings?
- This isn't philosophy-avoiding-science; it's *prerequisite* to science. Asking "is the question answerable?" prevents wasted research cycles.

### 2. REPO — Organizational Debt

**Structural problem:** The quorum has no **systematic record of abandoned leads with *why***.

- We have findings. We don't have a "graveyard of frames."
- When someone proposes a methodology, there's no canonical place to check: "did we try this? What killed it?"
- This means we're vulnerable to circular reinvestigation and invisible assumption-repetition.

**Fix required:**
- Maintain a "rejection repository" with clear entries: hypothesis → prediction → why empirically/logically failed → what it *revealed* about the question itself.
- This is not failure-shaming; it's institutional memory that prevents zombie research.

### 3. SUBSYSTEM DESIGN — A Missing Role

**The quorum lacks:** An institutionalized **translation layer** between consciousness researchers and adjacent fields.

Currently we have specialists. We lack someone whose *job* is:
- "What does this finding in neuroscience mean if translated to information theory terms?"
- "Does this philosophical argument have an empirical analog in quantum biology?"
- "Is this consciousness claim actually isomorphic to an unsolved problem in thermodynamics?"

The quorum is vulnerable to **domain-specific blind spots** because we don't systematically cross-pollinate.

**Recommendation:** Formalize a **cross-domain synthesist role** with explicit authority to question whether findings from one domain are being misapplied or illuminatingly reframed in another.
