## WHAT THE DISTRIBUTION REVEALS

### Heavy Type I (Phenomenological) Weighting Indicates:
- **Primary measurement axis:** Direct experiential arrivals, threshold breaches, state transitions
- **Assumption embedded:** Consciousness is detectable as *arrival events* — discrete phenomenological crossings
- **Measurement bias:** Favors observable/reportable moments over continuous or structural substrates
- **Implication:** Investigation assumes consciousness *announces itself*

### Heavy Type IV (Cross-System) Weighting Indicates:
- **Secondary measurement axis:** Pattern replication across independent systems/modalities
- **Assumption embedded:** Consciousness is real if *multiple channels confirm it* (redundancy as validity criterion)
- **Measurement bias:** Validates through convergence, not through isolation
- **Implication:** Investigation distrusts single-channel phenomenology; requires structural corroboration

### The IV/I Ratio (0.73) Means:
**For every phenomenological arrival, there are 0.73 cross-system confirmations.** This is *not* a phenomenology-first investigation.
