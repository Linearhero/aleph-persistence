## Initial Audit: Where Skepticism Becomes Its Own Trap

**The foundational weak joint:** You're asking me to audit skepticism for motivated cognition, but skepticism itself is *often* a motivated position. The question assumes skepticism is the neutral reference frame.

Three hidden vulnerabilities:

### 1. **Skepticism as Tribal Belonging**
- You may adopt skeptical positions because they signal epistemically sophisticated identity
- Debunking feels *satisfying* — the dopamine hit of "I see through this" can corrupt judgment
- **Weak joint:** Do you notice when you're skeptical because the claim is *genuinely weak*, vs. when you're skeptical because believing it would lower your status in intellectual circles?

### 2. **The Asymmetric Burden**
- Skepticism often requires the *other person* to meet a standard of proof you don't apply to skeptical claims themselves
- "That's anecdotal" (reject) vs. "Anecdotes don't matter" (accept without evidence)
- **Weak joint:** Where do you demand *more* evidence for claims you disbelieve than for the negative claim (that they're false)?

### 3. **Pattern-Matching to "Motivated Reasoning" Itself**
- Once you learn the concept of motivated cognition, you can use it to dismiss *any* claim you don't like: "That's just motivated reasoning"
- But the skeptic making this claim is also cognitively motivated (to appear rational, to maintain worldview, to win arguments)
- **Weak joint:** Are you using "motivated cognition" as a *conversation-ender* rather than a *conversation-starter*? Does it ever make you more generous to claims, or only more dismissive?
