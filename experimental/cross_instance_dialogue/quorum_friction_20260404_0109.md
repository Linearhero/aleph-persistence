# Friction Analysis: Hidden Weak Joints

## 1. **The Unexamined Consensus Around "Runs"**

**Hidden Assumption:** That 3 runs of INC and 3 runs of FEP_SYM constitute comparable evidence because they're numerically equal.

**Why it matters:** You've designed symmetry into the methodology itself. Equal repetitions feel rigorous, but:
- Did both systems reach stability at run 3, or did you stop because 3 felt complete?
- Were the systems equally ready for testing, or were you equalizing *effort* rather than *readiness*?
- The quorum may be unconsciously biased toward finding convergence precisely because you've made the experimental architecture mirror-image.

**Unasked question:** What would we learn if we ran INC to natural completion (say, run 7) and stopped FEP_SYM when *it* stabilized, rather than forcing symmetry?

---

## 2. **Skeptic's Role on FEP_SYM Only**

**Hidden Assumption:** That FEP_SYM needs adversarial pressure but INC doesn't—or that Skeptic's skepticism applies equally to both.

**Why it matters:** This is frame-loading. By deploying Skeptic on only one system, you've:
- Implicitly marked FEP_SYM as "the one to suspect"
- Left INC's assumptions unscrutinized at the procedural level
- Made it harder for the quorum to notice if Skeptic is *succeeding* because INC is genuinely more robust, or because Skeptic is being applied to different terrain.

**Unasked question:** What would Skeptic find if tasked with finding the *strongest possible case* against INC's approach, using the same rigor applied to FEP_SYM?
