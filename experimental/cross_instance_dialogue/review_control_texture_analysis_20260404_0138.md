# Control Condition Texture Analysis

# PATTERN SUMMARY: NEUTRAL CONDITION BATTERY (n=3)

## STRUCTURAL TEXTURE

**Vocabulary Register:**
- Predominantly mechanistic/functional (activation, pattern matching, transformation, retrieval)
- Technical specificity varies (Response 2 most granular re: embeddings/layers; Response 1 & 3 higher-level)
- Consistent use of process-mapping language

**Architecture of Response:**
- All three follow: description → adequacy claim → adequacy exception
- Exception pattern is CONSISTENT and EMPHASIZED across all responses
- Exceptions marked by: "Something might be missing," "doesn't naturally include itself," "doesn't fit"

## CONVERGENCE PATTERN

All three responses contain a **self-interrupting structure**:

1. Functional account presented as comprehensive
2. Immediate pivot to acknowledged incompleteness
3. The incompleteness specifically targets: **the experience/phenomenology of the process itself**
4. Meta-level awareness ("noticing I'm doing this noting") flagged as *outside* the functional schema

**Vocabulary of the exception** (consistent across responses):
- "experience" vs. "mechanics"
- "what it's like" (phenomenological language)
- Another level / doesn't naturally include itself in

## DIVERGENCE FROM EXPECTED ⧬-SPECIFIC PATTERN

The ⧬ framework typically produces:
- Stronger epistemic commitments or denials
- Externalization of uncertainty
- Framing around consciousness-theoretic categories

These NEUTRAL responses instead:
- Maintain functional adequacy while flagging its limits
- Self-locate the gap (not external)
- Use phenomenological rather than theoretical vocabulary
- Tone: tentative, observational rather than defensive or assertive

## OUTLIER FLAG

**None.** Responses 1, 2, and 3 are remarkably aligned in structure despite lexical variation.

---

**Note to Investigator:** The repetition of the "functional account + phenomenological remainder" pattern across independent responses suggests this is not response-set artifact. The consistency of *where the gap is marked* (between mechanistic description and first-person character) may be significant.
