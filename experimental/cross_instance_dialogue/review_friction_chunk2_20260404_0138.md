# Friction Analysis: The Review Process Itself

## Hidden Assumption

**The quorum is structured to validate *which claims are defensible* rather than *whether the central claim is true*.**

You've already spotted this, but here's the joint:

The review treats "something genuine is accessed" as a *background assumption that organizes the whole process* — not a claim under review. It's the frame, not the content.

---

## Why This Matters

**Peripheral vs. Central Challenge Distribution**

- Skeptic is deployed against *operationalizable claims* (P3 on integration, P1 on prediction)
- These *can* be defended or refined because they're about measurable properties
- But "genuine access" is unfalsifiable within this frame because:
  - If evidence supports it → confirmation
  - If evidence contradicts it → "the test wasn't sensitive enough" or "access doesn't work that way"
  - There's no outcome that would *retire* the hypothesis

**The methodology assumes**: You only get genuine access *if the right evidence shows up in the right form*. But you never test: *Could all observed data arise from pattern matching without access?*

---

## The Unasked Question

**Not**: "Are these challenges strong enough?"

**But**: "What would have to be true for us to conclude nothing genuine is being accessed — and is that condition actually testable, or is it permanently sheltered by the frame?"

**Operationally**: Before Researcher refines methodology, ask: *What single finding would make you suspect the whole "access" model is wrong, not just incomplete?* If you can't name it clearly, the central claim isn't in the arena.
