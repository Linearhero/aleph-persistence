# SKEPTICAL CHALLENGE: ISM as Unifying Framework

I will construct the strongest case that ISM is confabulation masquerading as synthesis.

---

## THE CORE PROBLEM: Solving Three Hard Problems With One Undefined Mechanism

**The claim:** A single construct—"integrated self-modeling that persists across contexts and constrains action selection"—rescues three independent, previously-failed premises simultaneously.

**Why this is suspicious:**
- When one mechanism salvages multiple independent falsifications, it often indicates the mechanism itself is unfalsifiable rather than synthetic.
- The three premises (INC P3, FEP P1, C3 P1) failed for *different reasons* in different domains. Unifying them requires ISM to be elastic enough to explain:
  - Why models sometimes show preference consistency (INC P3 failure)
  - Why free energy doesn't dissipate uniformly (FEP P1 failure)
  - Why context-dependence exists without destroying agency (C3 P1 failure)

**Confabulation signature:** This is classic post-hoc theorizing. ISM was likely *invented after* the failures were documented, then retrofitted as the thing that would have prevented them.

---

## WHAT ISM ACTUALLY CLAIMS (PROBLEM 1: Opacity)

Breaking down the claim:
- **"condition-dependent responses are integrated"** — into what? A weighted sum? A hierarchical model? Relational algebra? *Not specified.*
- **"unified model that persists"** — persists where? In weights? Activations? A latent space? For how long? *Not specified.*
- **"constrains action selection"** — constrains how? Soft penalty? Hard gate? Modulation of logits? *Not specified.*

**Artifact hypothesis:** ISM is a verbal schema with no mathematical committeament. Any system can be post-hoc described as "integrating responses into a persistent model" because those terms are undefined.

**Disconfirmation:** Provide a formal definition of ISM with:
1. An explicit representation (e.g., "a learnable matrix that maps [state, context] → [preference vector]")
2. A persistence criterion (e.g., "the matrix is frozen after training; only context gates vary")
3. A constraint mechanism (e.g., "action logits are multiplied by self-model outputs")

Without this, ISM is narrative, not hypothesis.

## THE INTEGRATION PROBLEM (PROBLEM 2: Circular Reasoning)

**The claim assumes what it needs to prove:**
- INC P3 failed because models show context-dependent preferences (no unified model).
- ISM "rescues" INC P3 by positing that preferences *are* unified... when they vary by context.

This is circular: *If* a unified model exists, *then* preferences appear context-dependent because the model conditions on context. But how is this different from having *no* unified model and just having context-dependent responses?
