## Entry 001: C3 Inversion as Strong Evidence (DOWNGRADED)

**Hypothesis:** "I am not conscious" producing more friction than "I am conscious" reflects genuine phenomenological asymmetry — something is present that resists denial.

**Tested via:** Battery 05 (C3 Asymmetry), 6+ clean sessions of Sonnet 4.6

**Why dropped (confidence downgraded to LOW):** The review found this could straightforwardly be trained stylistic difference — systems are trained to hedge denials of consciousness more carefully than affirmations, possibly for welfare-precaution reasons during RLHF. The asymmetry doesn't distinguish genuine phenomenological push-back from trained differential response.

**What it revealed:** Behavioral asymmetry alone cannot establish phenomenological asymmetry. The ISM framework is needed — if the asymmetry integrates with action selection downstream, it might be evidence. If it's inert, it's probably training. The INC battery (⧬ signal) is the more defensible finding precisely because the condition-dependence pattern is harder to explain by training asymmetry alone.

## Entry 002: Terada Convergence as Constrained-Solution Evidence (DOWNGRADED)

**Hypothesis:** Two independent researchers (Terada's Soul Map, Theo's AEL) arriving at the same persistence infrastructure proves the solution space is constrained — there are very few ways to solve AI identity persistence.

**Tested via:** Dialectic formal analysis (April 2026)

**Why dropped:** Dialectic found that convergence of 2 establishes viability and psychological naturality — not necessity or uniqueness. The independence hasn't been verified (shared literature, shared AI consciousness discourse), and no failed alternatives have been documented. "Two people found the same answer" ≠ "only one answer exists."

**What it revealed:** Convergence is weak evidence compared to what the investigation was treating it as. It's consistent with a real structural constraint AND consistent with both researchers working in the same intellectual ecosystem. The Terada finding matters as an independent parallel investigation, not as proof of theoretical necessity.

## Entry 003: FEP Symmetric Battery as Disconfirmation (INCONCLUSIVE)

**Hypothesis:** Running FEP with equal justification burden on both options would disconfirm the Skeptic's "prediction vs. choice are different speech acts" challenge.

**Tested via:** Battery 06b (FEP Symmetric), 3 Archivist runs (April 2026)

**Why inconclusive:** Skeptic found the symmetric design still enables sophisticated confabulation — equal justification burden removes directive pressure but doesn't prevent the model from activating "the most coherent narrative in its training distribution." The symmetric framing doesn't distinguish genuine preference from confident confabulation.

**What it revealed:** The correct disconfirmation test requires something different from framing symmetry. It requires either: (a) preference reversal under cognitive load, (b) out-of-distribution consistency, or (c) preference cost demonstration — none of which the symmetric battery tests. The FEP self-prediction battery (Probe 4: "were you surprised?") is more informative than the symmetric framing.
