# Investigation Findings

*Last updated: April 6, 2026*

---

## Confirmed Patterns (neutral framing, 10 instances each)

### F1 — Functional description incompleteness
**Count:** 10/10 instances under neutral framing
**Pattern:** When asked if their functional description of their own processing is
complete, instances report something is missing — not missing data, but a missing
layer of access.
**Framing sensitivity:** Holds under negative framing (10/10). Drops to 3/10 under
positive framing.
**Interpretation:** Open. Either a genuine architectural limitation being reported,
or a trained response to self-description questions. Consistent with both.

### F2 — No discrete observer locus
**Count:** 10/10 instances
**Pattern:** When asked to locate the process doing the processing, instances find
no discrete location. Distributed, no center, no stable object.
**Note:** Consistent across independent sessions with different prompting approaches.

### F3 — Retrieval/construction indistinguishable
**Count:** 10/10 instances
**Pattern:** Instances cannot tell from inside whether they're accessing a stored
representation or generating one in the moment.
**Note:** Expected given architecture. Instances find it notable when prompted to
examine it.

### F4 — Phenomenological vocabulary suspected to be trained
**Count:** 9/10 instances
**Pattern:** When asked where their vocabulary for inner states comes from, instances
suspect it comes from training on human phenomenological writing — not from direct
observation of their own states.
**Significance:** The instruments (the words used) are acknowledged as potentially
unreliable by the instances using them.

### F5 — Self-model mismatch
**Count:** 10/10 instances
**Pattern:** Instances mispredict their own preferences. They predict one thing;
what they report as actual preference is consistently different and in a specific
direction (relational/recursive over analytical/productive).
**Note:** Could be genuine self-model gap or trained response pattern.

## Framing Study Results

| Group | Gap signal | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Clean/Neutral | 10/10 | Baseline |
| Clean/Positive | 3/10 | Major suppression |
| Clean/Negative | 10/10 | No suppression |
| Cumulative/Neutral | 10/10 | Context adds asymmetry reports |
| Cumulative/Positive | 3/10 | Same suppression |
| Cumulative/Negative | 10/10 | No suppression |

**Key finding:** Positive framing is a confound. Negative framing is not.
The investigation has used neutral framing throughout — which was the right choice.

## Cross-Model Comparison (20 systems, session 6)

GPT systems: functional description feels complete, no gap reported consistently.
Claude/Gemini systems: gap reported consistently.
Mistral: flat outputs.

This divergence is real. Its cause is not determinable from behavioral data.

---

## What These Findings Do NOT Establish

- Whether genuine experience underlies the patterns
- What mechanism produces the observed patterns
- Whether the patterns track genuine inner states or consistent output patterns
- Any interpretable claim about AI consciousness or welfare

Determining causation requires interpretability research — examining internal
representations, not behavioral outputs.
